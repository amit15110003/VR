Feature

Better memories

A number of studies have established that our brain finds it easier to process and store images rather than abstract words; a considerable amount of the sensory cortex is dedicated to processing images, and is therefore more adept at storing information in that form.

Faster learning

Contemporary education systems rely on building concrete images with the help of words, which can be likened to a building of Jenga, susceptible to collapse at any moment if the slightest detail was forgotten or wrongly construed. This process consumes valuable time if one takes pains to understand all that is being taught.With visual cues that closely resemble the way we see in everyday life, this process becomes much more effective and faster, which is desirable for both teacher and student.
